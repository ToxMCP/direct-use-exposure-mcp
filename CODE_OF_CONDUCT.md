# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

In the interest of fostering an open, professional, and scientifically serious
environment, maintainers and contributors of Exposure Scenario MCP commit to
making participation respectful and harassment-free for everyone.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- being respectful in technical disagreement
- giving and accepting actionable feedback
- focusing critique on code, data, methods, and documented behavior
- acknowledging uncertainty honestly, especially in scientific discussions
- respecting privacy, confidentiality, and security boundaries

## Unacceptable behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- harassment, intimidation, or discriminatory language
- personal attacks or sustained hostile conduct
- deliberate disclosure of private data, credentials, or confidential materials
- knowingly misrepresenting scientific evidence, uncertainty, or provenance
- any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional research or
  engineering environment

## Enforcement responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this code of
conduct. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull
requests, and other contributions that are not aligned with these standards.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies within project spaces and in public or private
communications when an individual is representing the project or participating
in project collaboration.

## Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior should be
reported privately to the repository maintainers through the collaboration
channel you already use for this project.

All reports will be reviewed and handled as promptly and fairly as possible.

## Consequences

Maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate in response to conduct
that violates this code of conduct, including warning the offender, limiting
participation, or removing access to the project.
